# 04 — Circles

[← Back to manual](../README.md)

---

## What a Circle is

A Circle is a private safety group made up of people you trust. Everyone in a circle shares a level of safety awareness with each other — check-ins, nudges, SOS events, and location updates all flow through the circle.

You can be a member of more than one circle. One circle is always your **active circle** — the one that receives your check-ins and safety events.

---

## Viewing your active circle

Open the **Circles tab** (second icon in the tab bar) to see your currently active circle.

The main screen shows:

- Circle name and member count
- Member cards with their current status
- A mini map showing where members are
- Daily activity summary
- Quick access to the Circle Feed

`[SCREENSHOT: docs/screenshots/circles/circles-main.png]`

## Switching circles

If you are a member of more than one circle:

1. Tap the circle name at the top of the Circles tab
2. A sheet appears showing all your circles
3. Tap any circle to make it active

Your active circle is the one that receives your check-ins, nudges, and SOS alerts.

`[SCREENSHOT: docs/screenshots/circles/circles-switcher.png]`

---

## Creating a circle

1. Open the Circles tab
2. Tap the circle name → **Your circles**
3. Tap **Create circle**
4. Enter a circle name
5. Tap **Create**

Your new circle is created with you as the owner. Share the circle code or your username to invite members.

---

## Inviting someone to your circle

**Via circle code:**

1. Open the circle you want to share
2. Tap **Manage circle** or the settings icon
3. Tap **Invite members**
4. Share the circle code via message, AirDrop, or any other method

**Via username:**

You can also find and invite someone directly if you know their Obserc username.

---

## Joining a circle

If someone shares a circle code or invite link with you:

1. Open the Circles tab
2. Tap **Join a circle**
3. Enter the circle code
4. Tap **Join**

You will be added immediately and can start seeing the circle's activity feed.

---

## Understanding circle summary cards

Each member in your circle is shown as a card with:

- Their name and profile photo
- Their current safety status
- Their last known location or last active time
- A nudge shortcut

---

## The Circle Feed

The Circle Feed is the activity inbox for your circle. It shows a running log of:

- Check-ins from circle members
- Nudges sent and received
- SOS events (active and resolved)
- Night Out session activity
- Join and invite notifications

**To open the Circle Feed:**

Tap **Feed** on the Circles tab main screen, or tap the feed icon in the top navigation.

`[SCREENSHOT: docs/screenshots/circles/circle-feed-inbox.png]`

### Feed item types

| Type | What it shows |
|------|---------------|
| **Check-in** | A member confirmed they are okay, with optional note and location |
| **Nudge** | A "are you okay?" sent between members |
| **SOS** | An active or resolved SOS event with location |
| **Night Out** | Session started, ended, or invite received |
| **Join** | A new member joined the circle |

### Feed filters

Tap the filter pills at the top of the feed to show only specific event types.

`[SCREENSHOT: docs/screenshots/circles/circle-feed-filters.png]`

### Active SOS in the feed

If a circle member has an active SOS, it appears pinned at the top of the feed with their live location and a prompt to acknowledge.

`[SCREENSHOT: docs/screenshots/circles/circle-feed-sos-active.png]`

---

## Managing your circle

### As a member

- You can leave a circle at any time via **Manage circle → Leave circle**

### As the circle owner

Tap **Manage circle** to access owner-only controls:

- **Rename circle** — update the circle name
- **Remove a member** — remove someone from the circle
- **Delete circle** — permanently delete the circle and all its history

`[SCREENSHOT: docs/screenshots/circles/circles-manage.png]`

`[SCREENSHOT: docs/screenshots/circles/circles-members.png]`

> **Note:** Deleting a circle cannot be undone. All feed history will be lost.
